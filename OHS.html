Occupational Health Standards

Public health has a division called occupational health and safety that works to constantly improve standards for workplace health and safety. It examines trends in worker illnesses and injuries and makes recommendations for reducing the dangers and risks. Occupational health is a very broad umbrella. Infection control, environmental industry standards, and strategies for handling workplace violence are all covered in addition to first aid procedures and the safe operation of large machinery.

Fires🔥

Eu1-Pixabay-CC-Public Domain

All structures used for occupation must be situated in areas permitted by the locality's appropriate zoning regulations in order to comply with fire health standards. The Fire Code of the Philippines must be followed when conducting fire tests on the construction materials and fire safety gear used in any workplace. Standards outlined in Chapter 9 of the Philippine Society of Mechanical Engineers (PSME) Code must be followed for the design and implementation of indoor and outdoor general storage, sprinkler systems, and fire prevention systems. Every floor and basement of every business shall have a minimum of two exits capable of vacating the work area in five (5) minutes.

EarthQuakes🏡

Angelo_Giodorno-Pixabay-CC-Public Domain

When an earthquake occurs a densely populated area, it may result in fatalities, serious injuries, and significant property damage. Every company owes it to its workers to maintain a disaster plan. It is necessary to plan an emergency evacuation place, a nearby safe space-ideally outside-where employees can gather in the event of a fire or earthquake. It ought to be outside, far from any structures or electricity lines.

Tropical Cyclones🌀

WikiMedia-Pixabay-CC-Public Domain

Tropical cyclones such as typhoons have the ability to completely destroy towns and cities. It's important to be aware of your local community's emergency plans, warning signs, and shelters in addition to having an evacuation plan in place and being aware with the terminologies used to describe storm warnings. To guarantee that all employees are aware of what to do in an emergency the company should conduct training and drills. The need of pre-incident training for both employees and employers should be emphasized in the program and must be based on the lessons discovered during drills, revised plans and procedures.
